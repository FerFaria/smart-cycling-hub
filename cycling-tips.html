Cycling Tips

20 essential tips to help you ride safer, smarter, and stronger.

1

Always Wear a Helmet

A properly fitted helmet is your most important piece of safety equipment. Replace it after any impact.

2

Check Tyre Pressure

Correct tyre pressure improves efficiency and prevents flats. Check before every ride.

3

Use Hand Signals

Signal turns and stops clearly to communicate with drivers and other cyclists.

4

Stay Visible

Wear bright clothing and use front/rear lights, especially in low-light conditions.

5

Maintain Your Brakes

Test brakes before every ride. Replace worn pads and keep cables properly tensioned.

6

Learn Basic Repairs

Know how to fix a flat tyre and adjust your chain. Carry a basic repair kit on rides.

7

Hydrate Regularly

Drink water before, during, and after rides. Dehydration affects performance and safety.

8

Ride Predictably

Maintain a steady line and avoid sudden movements. Be consistent so others can anticipate your actions.

9

Obey Traffic Rules

Follow the same rules as motor vehicles. Stop at red lights and yield where required.

10

Warm Up Properly

Start with easy pedalling for 5–10 minutes to prepare muscles and joints for harder effort.

11

Adjust Your Saddle Height

Proper saddle height prevents knee pain and improves pedalling efficiency. Your leg should be almost fully extended at the bottom of the pedal stroke.

12

Use Gears Efficiently

Shift to easier gears for hills and harder gears for flats. Maintain a cadence of 70–90 RPM.

13

Plan Your Route

Know your route before you ride. Use cycling-specific maps and apps to find bike-friendly roads.

14

Eat for Energy

Fuel with carbohydrates before long rides. Bring snacks for rides over 60 minutes.

15

Ride with Others

Group rides improve safety and motivation. Learn group riding etiquette and communication.

16

Protect Against Weather

Layer clothing for cold rides, use sunscreen in summer, and carry a rain jacket for unpredictable weather.

17

Lock Your Bike Securely

Use a quality U-lock through the frame and rear wheel. Lock to a fixed object in a visible area.

18

Stretch After Riding

Cool down with gentle stretches for your legs, back, and shoulders to prevent stiffness.

19

Track Your Progress

Use a cycling computer or app to monitor distance, speed, and improvement over time.

20

Enjoy the Ride

Cycling is about fun and freedom. Explore new routes, set personal goals, and enjoy the journey.
