Keep Rolling

Maintenance Guide

10 essential maintenance tips for each bike type. City/Commuter is free; other types require a premium subscription.

  1. Check Tyre Pressure WeeklyCity tyres should be inflated to 60–80 PSI. Under-inflated tyres increase rolling resistance and puncture risk.
  2. Lubricate the Chain MonthlyApply wet or dry lube depending on weather. Wipe excess with a clean rag to prevent grime buildup.
  3. Inspect Brake PadsCheck brake pad wear indicators. Replace when the grooves are no longer visible. Test stopping power regularly.
  4. Keep the Drivetrain CleanDegrease and clean the chain, cassette, and chainring every 2–4 weeks depending on usage.
  5. Tighten Bolts and FastenersCheck handlebar, stem, seat post, and wheel axle bolts monthly. Use a torque wrench for carbon components.
  6. Check Lights and ReflectorsEnsure front and rear lights work. Replace batteries or charge regularly. Clean reflectors for visibility.
  7. Inspect Tyres for WearLook for cuts, embedded glass, or worn tread. Replace tyres when the tread is smooth or sidewalls show damage.
  8. Adjust Saddle and HandlebarsEnsure proper riding position. Saddle height should allow a slight bend in the knee at the bottom of the pedal stroke.
  9. Clean the Bike RegularlyWash with mild soap and water. Avoid high-pressure washers which can force water into bearings.
  10. Annual Professional ServiceTake your bike for a full service annually. A mechanic can spot issues you might miss and ensure everything is properly adjusted.

Electric Bike maintenance tips require a premium subscription.

  1. Monitor Battery HealthCharge between 20–80% for optimal battery longevity. Avoid storing fully depleted or fully charged.
  2. Keep Electrical Contacts CleanClean battery and motor connectors with a dry cloth. Prevent corrosion with dielectric grease.
  3. Update Firmware RegularlyCheck the manufacturer's app for motor firmware updates that improve performance and efficiency.
  4. Inspect Motor Mounting BoltsVibration can loosen motor bolts. Check torque specs monthly, especially for mid-drive motors.
  5. Check Sensor AlignmentSpeed and cadence sensors must be properly aligned. Misalignment causes erratic pedal assist behaviour.
  6. Protect from WaterWhile most e-bikes are water-resistant, avoid submerging the motor or battery. Dry after wet rides.
  7. Inspect Wiring HarnessCheck cables for chafing, loose connections, or damage. Secure loose wires away from moving parts.
  8. Brake Pads Wear FasterE-bikes are heavier and faster. Check brake pads more frequently than on regular bikes.
  9. Tyre Pressure for E-BikesE-bike tyres need slightly higher pressure (70–90 PSI) due to the extra weight of the motor and battery.
  10. Store Battery ProperlyIn winter, remove the battery and store at room temperature. Keep charge level around 50% during long storage.

Mountain Bike maintenance tips require a premium subscription.

  1. Service Suspension RegularlyClean stanchions after every ride. Full suspension service every 50–100 hours of riding.
  2. Check Tubeless SealantTop up sealant every 2–3 months. Shake wheels to check if sealant is still liquid inside.
  3. Inspect Dropper PostKeep the dropper post clean and lubricated. Service the cartridge annually or when action becomes sluggish.
  4. Clean After Every Muddy RideMud accelerates wear on all components. Rinse the bike gently and dry the chain after muddy trails.
  5. Check Disc Brake RotorsInspect for warping and contamination. Minimum rotor thickness is usually 1.5mm — replace when worn.
  6. Tighten Through-AxlesCheck front and rear through-axle tightness before every ride. Loose axles affect handling and safety.
  7. Inspect Frame for CracksAfter hard impacts, check the frame around welds and pivot points for hairline cracks.
  8. Replace Chain RegularlyMountain bike chains stretch faster due to mud and grit. Replace every 1,500–2,000 km to protect the cassette.
  9. Check Spoke TensionTrail riding loosens spokes. Squeeze pairs of spokes to check tension and true wheels as needed.
  10. Adjust Suspension SettingsSet sag, rebound, and compression for your weight and riding style. Re-check after significant weight changes.

Road Bike maintenance tips require a premium subscription.

  1. Maintain High Tyre PressureRoad tyres need 80–130 PSI. Check before every ride as pressure drops faster in thin tyres.
  2. Wax Your ChainWaxed chains run cleaner and last longer than lubed chains. Re-wax every 300–500 km.
  3. Check Bar Tape ConditionReplace worn or torn bar tape for comfort and grip. Clean regularly to prevent slipping.
  4. Inspect Carbon ComponentsCheck carbon handlebars, seatpost, and frame for impact damage. Use a torque wrench — never over-tighten.
  5. Align Derailleurs PreciselyRoad shifting must be crisp. Adjust cable tension and limit screws for perfect gear changes.
  6. Replace Brake Cables AnnuallyCables stretch and fray over time. Fresh cables improve braking response significantly.
  7. Clean Rim Brake TracksIf using rim brakes, clean the braking surface with alcohol to remove rubber deposits and improve stopping.
  8. Check Wheel TruenessSpin each wheel and check for wobble. True wheels reduce drag and improve handling at speed.
  9. Service Bottom BracketListen for creaking or grinding. Service or replace the bottom bracket every 5,000–10,000 km.
  10. Inspect Cleats and PedalsCheck cleat wear and float adjustment. Worn cleats can cause knee pain and unclipping issues.

Gravel Bike maintenance tips require a premium subscription.

  1. Maintain Tubeless SetupCheck sealant levels monthly. Inspect tyre beads for proper seating. Carry a plug kit for trail-side repairs.
  2. Lower Tyre Pressure for GripRun 30–45 PSI for gravel. Lower pressure improves traction on loose surfaces but increases pinch flat risk.
  3. Clean After Gravel RidesDust and fine grit penetrate everywhere. Rinse the bike and re-lube the chain after every gravel ride.
  4. Check Frame MountsGravel bikes have many bottle cage and rack mounts. Check bolt tightness as vibration loosens them.
  5. Inspect Disc Brakes FrequentlyGravel riding wears brake pads faster than road. Check pad thickness and rotor condition regularly.
  6. Service Headset BearingsRough terrain stresses headset bearings. Check for play and re-grease every 3–6 months.
  7. Protect the ChainstayInstall a chainstay protector to prevent chain slap damage on rough terrain.
  8. Check Wheel BearingsSpin wheels and feel for roughness. Gravel riding accelerates bearing wear — service annually.
  9. Carry Spare PartsPack a spare tube, tyre boot, multi-tool, and chain links for remote gravel adventures.
  10. Adjust Fit for Long RidesGravel rides are often longer. Fine-tune saddle position and handlebar reach for all-day comfort.
